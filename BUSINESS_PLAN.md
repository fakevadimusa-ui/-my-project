## Lead Generation — How You Find Deals

### Tier 1: Free (do these daily)
- **Driving for dollars** — Drive 65802/65803. Look for: overgrown grass, boarded windows, tarped roofs, peeling paint, newspapers piling up. Log every address in DealMachine.
- **Cold calling** — Pull lists from county records or DealMachine. Call absentee owners, tax-delinquent owners, vacant properties.
- **Facebook groups** — Post weekly in Springfield REI groups. Sellers sometimes reach out.
- **Craigslist** — Check "for sale by owner" listings. Distressed sellers often post there.

### Tier 2: Paid ($400 budget)
- **Direct mail** — Send 60–100 postcards/month to your lists (absentee owners, tax delinquent, probate). Simple message: "I buy houses cash, any condition, fast close. Call Vadim: [your number]"
- **Skip tracing** — Use DealMachine to find phone numbers for absentee owners you can't reach.

### Lead Priority List (most motivated sellers)
1. Tax-delinquent owners (desperate, behind on payments)
2. Absentee owners (don't live there, don't care about it)
3. Probate / inherited properties (heirs want cash, not a house)
4. Pre-foreclosure (need out fast)
5. Vacant / distressed (visible problems = motivated seller)

## Deal Process — Step by Step

### Step 1: Find the lead
Driver for dollars, cold call list, direct mail response, Facebook

### Step 2: Qualify the seller (on the phone)
Ask:
- "What's going on with the property?"
- "Are you looking to sell?"
- "What would you need to walk away happy?"
- "How soon do you need to move?"
- "Is there a mortgage on it?"

**You're looking for: motivation + timeline + flexibility on price**

### Step 3: Run your numbers
```
ARV (what it'll sell for fixed up)
× 0.70
− Estimated repairs
− Your assignment fee ($5K–$10K)
= Your Maximum Allowable Offer (MAO)
```

Use Zillow, Redfin, and Realtor.com for comps.
If you can't estimate repairs yet: use $20–$30/sqft for light rehab, $40–$60 for heavy.

### Step 4: Make the offer
Be direct. Be calm. Don't apologize.
> "Based on what I'm seeing and the repairs needed, I can offer you $[X]. I can close in 2–3 weeks, cash, no agents, no fees."

### Step 5: Get it under contract
Use a standard Missouri Purchase and Sale Agreement.
Make sure it includes: assignment clause ("and/or assigns"), inspection period, and earnest money ($100–$500 is fine).

### Step 6: Find your buyer
Send the deal to your cash buyer list. Include:
- Address
- Asking price (your contract price + your fee)
- ARV
- Estimated repairs
- Photos

### Step 7: Assign the contract
Use an Assignment of Contract form.
Buyer pays your assignment fee at closing — you never own the house.

### Step 8: Close
Title company or real estate attorney handles it.
You get paid. Log the deal. Move to the next one.

## Seller Script (short version)

> "Hey, my name is Vadim — I buy houses in Springfield for cash. I came across your property on [address] and wanted to see if you'd have any interest in a cash offer. I close fast, no agents, no fees. Is that something you'd be open to talking about?"

**If yes:** go into qualify questions above.
**If no:** "No problem at all — mind if I check back in a few months in case anything changes?"
**If hostile:** hang up politely. Move on. Never argue.

## Mindset Rules

1. **Volume beats perfection.** A mediocre script dialed 100 times beats a perfect script dialed 5 times.
2. **Follow up relentlessly.** 80% of deals come from follow-up, not first contact. Call back in 2 weeks. Then again in 30 days.
3. **The money is in the motivation, not the property.** A beautiful house with a non-motivated seller is worthless to you. An ugly house with a desperate seller is a deal.
4. **Your first deal will not be perfect.** Take it anyway. Learn from it. The confidence from closing one deal is worth more than 6 months of studying.
5. **Treat this like a job, not a hobby.** 10 dials a day, every day, even when you don't feel like it.
